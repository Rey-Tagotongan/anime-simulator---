Scamming

Scamming is the process of a person making fraudulent claims or offers to trade champions and not holding up their end of the deal. This is commonly done as an easy way to acquire champions.

  • Do not scam others. There are several types of scams:
  • Trade Scam: The scammer asks the victim to trade their offer first, then takes the item and leaves without completing the trade.
  • "Can I Borrow?" Scam: The scammer asks to "test" the champion and then runs away with it.
  • Friend Betrayal Scam: Someone pretends to be your friend to gain your trust, then asks to "test" your champion and disappears with it.
  • Potential Scams: Any trade that cannot be completed immediately, such as gifting shards/yen, helping with boss powers, finding devil fruits, or training others, may be considered crosstrading, which can lead to a ban.
  • Inventory Limitations: Ensure you have enough space for trades, as limited inventory can cause failed trades.
  • Fighting Pass Champions Are Non-Tradeable: This includes Kirito, Asuna, Gon, Meliodas, Shinra, Mihawk, Escanor, Zenitsu, Bakugo, Rock Lee, Marco, and Ochaco.
  • Do not beg for champions. There are many ways to obtain them, such as trading, grinding chikara, or using codes. Begging is considered similar to scamming.
