Beware of Scamming

Scamming is when a player deceives others by making false promises in trades or requests, usually to obtain champions unfairly. Protect yourself and others by understanding the common types of scams and how to avoid them.

Common Types of Scams

  • 1. Trade Scam: The scammer convinces the victim to send their item first but does not return the agreed trade.
  • 2. "Can I Borrow?" Scam: The scammer asks to test or borrow a champion and never returns it.
  • 3. Fake Friend Scam: Someone pretends to be your friend, gains your trust, then asks for a trade and disappears.
  • 4. Fake Middleman Scam: A scammer pretends to be a trusted middleman but takes the items and runs.
  • 5. Impersonation Scam: The scammer pretends to be a mod or admin, claiming they can help you trade but steals your items.
  • 6. ‚ÄúLimited Time Deal‚Äù Scam: The scammer pressures you to trade quickly, claiming the offer is only available for a short time.
  • 7. External Trade Scam: Scammers ask you to trade outside the game (e.g., for Robux or real money), which is risky and may get you banned.

Important Rules

  • Inventory Limitations: Ensure you have enough space before trading, as failed trades due to a full inventory are not refunded.
  • Fighting Pass Champions Are Non-Tradeable: The following champions cannot be traded: Kirito, Asuna, Gon, Meliodas, Shinra, Mihawk, Escanor, Zenitsu, Bakugo, Rock Lee, Marco, and Ochaco.
  • Do Not Beg for Champions: There are legitimate ways to obtain them, such as grinding chikara, using codes, or trading fairly. Begging is considered disruptive and may lead to penalties.
